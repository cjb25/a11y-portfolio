What is A11y?

"A11y" is short for "accessibility." There are 11 letters between the "A" and the "Y", and developers use it as shorthand. Accessibile means websites can be used by everyone, including people who are blind, visually impaired, use screen readers, or have other access needs.

Why It Matters

I'm a legally blind developer-in-training, so this isn't just something I'm passionate about, it's my way of life. This portfolio is a living demonstration for my peers, instructors, and future collaborators and employers. I am transparent with my lived experience, hoping to increase awareness and show how accessibility can be meaningful, practical, and creative.

Try It Yourself

As you explore my site, you'll learn several ways to test accessibility using the tools I am most familiar with - JAWS, NVDA, VoiceOver, as well as keyboard navigation. You'll have the opportunity to experience firsthand the importance of building more inclusive designs from the start.
