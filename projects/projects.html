Course: Introduction to programming

Week 1: Box Model

One of my earliest assignments asked me to explore how inline and block elements behave in the box model. My instructor encouraged me to experiment with borders and spacing to see the visual layout more clearly.

These visual cues were not accessibile to me in the traditional way. I relied on trial, logic, and support. I experimented with red borders so I could faintly detect them. I used Be My Eyes to borrow sighted guidance, and I asked thoughtful follow-up questions when I couldnt confirm what I had changed.

This beginner project is a great example of how important it is to structure layouts that work for everyone, including the deceloper behind the screen.

Accessible Exploration

After learning more about accessibility, I revistied this project to improve its structure for keyboard users an screen readers. This version includes clearer focusable elements, a skip to main content link, and improved semantic HTML. Making these changes helped me connect thhe technical side of accessibility with the lived experience of using assistive technology, and why it matters.

I then rebuilt the project to include accessible focusable components, ARIA attributes, and a "Skip to main content".

Try it yourself: View my updated, accessible version on CodePen

Use your keyboard to navigate:

  • Press Tab to move forward through links, buttons, and form elements.
  • Use Shift+ Tab to move backward.
  • When a link is focused, press Enter to activate.

As you tab through, notice how the "Skip to main content" link appears first and the headigns and links follow a logical, usable order. This helps people like me who rely on keyboards or screen readers have the same access as mouse users.
